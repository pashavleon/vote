World Cup 2026 opening ceremonies: three cities, one tournament

Updated 11 June 2026 · TOP FAN VOTE editorial · Draft

For the first time, the FIFA World Cup will launch with three separate opening ceremonies — one in each host nation. If you are planning to be in the stadium, the practical details matter more than showbiz: there is no standalone ceremony ticket, and each celebration is tied to that country’s opening match.

Tickets: ceremony access is included

FIFA does not sell a separate “opening ceremony only” ticket. Entry to the ceremony is included with your match ticket for the corresponding opening game. Official sales run through FIFA.com/tickets and the Last-Minute Sales Phase (first-come, first-served while stocks last).

Indicative primary-market prices reported before kickoff (vary by category and availability):

  • Mexico City — Category 4 from roughly $370; premium categories significantly higher.
  • Toronto — Category 2 around $2,300; Category 1 above $3,100 in late sales.
  • Los Angeles — Category 4 from roughly $560; hospitality packages $6,000+.

Resale is only safe through FIFA’s official resale/exchange marketplace. Third-party sites often charge large premiums and may not deliver valid tickets.

Getting there: taxi, transit & timing

Mexico City (Estadio Azteca)

  • Allow 2–3 hours before kick-off for security queues at a 87,000-seat venue.
  • Metro Line 2 (Taxqueña) + light rail to Estadio Azteca is the usual budget option; ride-hail and regulated taxis are common from central neighborhoods — agree route or use app pricing.
  • Traffic on Tlalpan corridor can spike on matchdays; many fans pre-book return taxis away from the stadium exits.

Toronto (BMO Field)

  • Exhibition Place is walkable from Union Station via streetcar routes; GO Transit serves regional fans.
  • Uber/Lyft pickup zones are designated on busy nights — expect surge pricing after the match.

Los Angeles (SoFi Stadium)

  • SoFi is in Inglewood; Metro K Line (Hawthorne/Inglewood) helps but many fans use ride-hail from downtown LA (45–90 min depending on traffic).
  • Parking is limited and pre-sold; ride-hail often drops at official zones — follow stadium signage.

Historical context

Previous World Cups used a single opening ceremony, often at the first match of the tournament. The 2026 format reflects the first tri-host World Cup since the expanded 48-team, 104-match edition. Mexico previously opened the 1970 and 1986 tournaments at the Azteca; the US hosted in 1994 without a Mexico City ceremony; Canada is hosting for the first time.

The global opener — Mexico vs South Africa on 11 June — carries extra emotional weight: South Africa return to the Azteca 16 years after their 2010 opener, and Mexico welcome the world on home soil.

What to expect in the stadium

  • Pre-match fan zones, music, and host-city programming before the scripted ceremony.
  • Security rules: check FIFA’s Stadium Code of Conduct for bag size, prohibited items, and water-bottle policy (US/Canada venues allow one sealed disposable bottle; reusable hard bottles are banned).
  • June heat: hydration breaks are scheduled in matches; fans should plan for sun and late-afternoon temperatures in several US venues.
