Who got it right? WC 2026 fan prediction scorecard

Updated 22 Jun 2026 · 31 finished group matches · unofficial poll, not FIFA

Fans on TOP FAN VOTE pick a winner (or draw) before each group game. Once the score is in, we count how many votes matched the actual result. This is live data from our poll — not betting advice, not a bookmaker line.

The numbers

48.6% of votes called the right outcome

  • 593 votes across 31 finished group-stage matches (31 with at least one vote).
  • 288 correct predictions — about 48.6% overall accuracy.
  • Crowd majority (the option with the highest share) matched the result on 19 of 31 matches (61.3%).

In plain language: the “wisdom of the crowd” is decent on obvious home wins, but upsets and tight draws still burn a lot of picks.

Where the crowd whiffed

Biggest misses so far

  • ESP vs CPV (0–0) — 93.3% picked Spain; actual: Draw.
  • QAT vs SUI (1–1) — 90.5% picked Switzerland; actual: Draw.
  • POR vs COD (1–1) — 88.9% picked Portugal; actual: Draw.

These are the games where the plurality of fans backed the wrong side — often because a favourite looked safe on paper.

Betting context

Fan picks vs bookmaker odds — same traps?

Search traffic around the World Cup is not only vote and poll — it is World Cup 2026 odds, betting tips, match predictions, and who will win futures. Our own Google data still shows almost all clicks on poll-intent queries; betting phrases have not landed on topfan.vote yet. This section is for fans comparing free picks to what bookmakers price — not a tip sheet.

Bookmakers blend models with public money: heavy volume on favourites (England, Brazil, hosts) can shorten odds beyond “true” probability. Sound familiar? Our crowd did the same — piling onto Spain, Portugal, Switzerland, Uruguay — and still whiffed when the result was a draw.

FixtureScoreFan leanWhat markets signalled
ESP vs CPV0–0 Draw 93% Spain Spain opened as a massive favourite (widely quoted around −1200 pre-match). Almost nobody — fans or casual bettors — was pricing a Cape Verde shutout.
QAT vs SUI1–1 Draw 91% Switzerland Switzerland were clear favourites in match markets; draw was the third leg most tickets ignored.
POR vs COD1–1 Draw 89% Portugal Portugal carried short win odds; our voters mirrored that — 0% correct on the draw.

Implied probability from odds is not the same as our vote share — bookies build in margin, and we count one fan one pick. But the pattern matches: favourites feel safe, draws punish both crowds and liability-heavy markets. On three-way football, even sharp books rarely “beat” random guessers every week; they manage margin and volume.

Bottom line

Ready to put real money with bookmakers?

Our fans — voting for free, with nothing at stake except pride — still got only 48.6% of outcomes right. The majority pick was wrong on 12 of 31 finished games. The painful misses were not wild long shots; they were “obvious” favourites and ignored draws.

If the crowd cannot call Spain at 93% or Switzerland at 91% — are you sure you want to stake cash on the same story the market is already selling?

A free fan poll costs nothing when you are wrong. A losing bet does not. If this scorecard reads uncomfortably — good. Use it to sanity-check hype, then vote for fun on upcoming matches without touching a wallet.

Method

How we count

One vote per match per browser. “Correct” means your pick equals the match outcome (home win, draw, or away win) after the game is marked finished in our database. Percentages are vote-weighted, not per-user averages.

Bookmaker lines in the table above are described from widely reported pre-match markets, not live feeds from a single operator. A future update may add closing implied probability per match from public odds archives.
